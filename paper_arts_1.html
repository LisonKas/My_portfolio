PAPER ARTS

PAINTING, WATERCOLOR,
MARKERS, SKETCH

On this page, there are many types of paper art, but mostly paintings, as I fell in love with it some months ago. That is why I am painting many different things to try new techniques. Most of these artworks are illustrations from my original story.

This blonde, angel-like lady is a character from my graphic novel project. I painted her with acrylics — the first type of paint I tried.

These are gouache paintings. My first objective was to improve my ability to paint human beings and emotions. In the last one, I enjoyed working on the reflections of the armor!

Here are some alcohol marker works. Three of them were made for the infamous Inktober event. The last one is one of my first studies of lighting with markers!

ALCOHOL
MARKERS

Beside, you can see three landscape paintings. The first one is in acrylic, and the other two are in gouache. I use a bright magenta layer before painting the rest of the drawing. It helps brighten the colors I apply afterward. That’s why you can see some parts of the painting with a pink reflection.

My work is largely inspired by my journeys and the environments I experience.

ACRYLICS, GOUACHE

WATERCOLOR

I really enjoy using watercolor to create character illustrations for my graphic novel.

In these, I hide many details and hints about their story — their present, and sometimes even their future. Some are human, others are not, and each atmosphere reveals aspects of their personality and their struggles.

Watercolor is a medium I explored long before more traditional painting, as I’ve always loved its visual possibilities. I also enjoy combining it with liner work.

Architecture is another passion of mine, and I’m still exploring how to integrate it into intricate, detailed landscapes.
